TX19 - Asking for a clearance certificate

check

Before you begin

A clearance certificate allows the legal representative to distribute assets without the risk of being held personally responsible for unpaid amounts the person who died, estate, trust, or corporation might owe the CRA. The legal representative’s financial institution or lawyers may also ask them for a clearance certificate.

  • A complete and signed copy of the will, including any codicils, renunciations, disclaimers, and probate documents if applicable
  • All documents required to verify the legal representative
  • A detailed list and description of the assets that were owned by the person who died at the date of death, including:
    • All assets that were held jointly
    • All registered retirement savings plans and registered retirement income funds (including those with a named or designated beneficiary)
    • The adjusted cost base (ACB) and fair market value (FMV) at the date of distribution by the estate for all assets
    • Total value of the estate’s assets
  • Schedule 3 – Capital Gains (or Losses) from the final T1 income tax return
  • A detailed statement of distribution of the estate’s assets to date
  • A statement of proposed distribution for any holdback or residual amount/property
  • A list of the names, addresses, and tax identification numbers, such as social insurance numbers, of any beneficiaries of property other than cash

If documents related to this request were submitted previously, you will be required to provide the reference number and/or confirmation number.
